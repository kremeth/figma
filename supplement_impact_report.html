Bottom line

Cardiovascular and performance markers moved up fast — now the next unlock is sleep quality.

This month brought some very encouraging progress. Recovery improved, VO₂ max moved up, workout effort increased strongly, and resting heart rate moved lower. That is a strong pattern overall, and it suggests the stack is already creating meaningful momentum. CoQ10 and Omega-3 in particular look well aligned with the gains showing up on the cardiovascular and performance side.

The main constraint now is that sleep-stage quality did not improve enough. Deep sleep needs to move up, REM has not improved enough relative to total sleep, and light sleep is still taking up too much of the night. In practical terms, that means the next intervention should be judged primarily on whether it can improve sleep architecture rather than on whether it can add more cardiovascular upside.

From a stack decision point of view, CoQ10 and Omega-3 still look correctly placed and should be given more time to compound. Magnesium is the least convincing part of the current stack because, by this stage, you would usually want to see a clearer sleep-related signal if it were the right lever. That makes it the most logical candidate to change first.

So the month 2 procedure is straightforward: keep the ingredients that are already aligned with the observed cardiovascular response, change the part of the stack that has not translated into enough sleep-stage improvement, and evaluate the next cycle primarily against deep sleep, REM share, and light sleep share. If those move in the right direction, your overall metric health score will be a natural reflection of that.
