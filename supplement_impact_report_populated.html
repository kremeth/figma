Bottom line

Performance markers moved up fast. Now the next unlock is sleep quality.

Recovery improved and workout intensity rose significantly — conditions that typically push recovery scores down. They went up instead. That is the most meaningful signal in the report, and it points to CoQ10 doing exactly what it is supposed to do. VO2 Max edged up on limited data. The performance and cardiovascular side of the stack is working.

The main constraint is sleep fragmentation, not sleep depth alone. Awake time doubled from 4% to 8% of total sleep. Disturbances increased from 3 to around 5 per night. REM reversed direction after day 26. These are signatures of cortisol-driven arousal under elevated training load — a mechanism no supplement in the current stack directly targets.

The Magnesium swap is not about clear failure. The deep sleep decline actually slowed in the supplement window — from −0.22 to −0.02 pp/day — which may be a partial signal. But the pattern that worsened most points to cortisol-driven fragmentation, and Magnesium does not address that. Ashwagandha targets it by reducing cortisol directly. Vitamin D targets it by reinforcing the circadian signal that tells your body when to enter restorative sleep. Which one fits depends on how the broken sleep actually feels to you.

Month 2 is a targeted test: CoQ10 and Omega-3 keep running, Probiotics gets its full evaluation window, and Ashwagandha takes on the sleep fragmentation problem directly. Judge month 2 primarily on awake time, disturbances per night, and deep sleep share. If those three move in the right direction, the overall score follows naturally.
